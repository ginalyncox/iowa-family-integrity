Press Overview

The Initiative aligns Iowa’s medical-cannabis framework with child-welfare practice so lawful patients are never penalized for responsible treatment. Evidence of impairment, not the mere presence of metabolites, is the standard.

Quick Facts

Name
Iowa Family Integrity and Patient Protection Initiative
Founder
Gina Lyn Cox — Altoona, Iowa (patient-rights advocate)
Core Goal
Safeguard lawful medical-cannabis patients from discrimination in child-custody, foster-care, and adoption proceedings.
Standard
Use impairment—not the mere presence of THC metabolites—as the evidence-based safety test.
Scope
Legislative amendments; Iowa HHS administrative guidance; and judicial education to align courtroom evaluations with Iowa’s medical-cannabis law.
Purpose
Harmonize medical-cannabis policy with family-protection statutes so compassion, evidence, and parental rights coexist under law.

Collaboration & Sponsorship

The Initiative welcomes dialogue with Iowa legislators interested in sponsoring or supporting this reform, as well as legal, medical, and community partners working to align family-welfare policy with Iowa’s medical-cannabis law.

Boilerplate (for articles)

The Iowa Family Integrity & Patient Protection Initiative is a citizen-led effort to align Iowa child-welfare practice with state medical-cannabis law. The Initiative advances a clear standard—impairment, not presence—through legislative language, HHS guidance, and judicial education.
