Impairment, not presence.

Lawful medical-cannabis patients should never be penalized in custody, foster-care, or adoption decisions. We’re aligning Iowa statute, HHS guidance, and courtroom practice to keep families intact.

Quick Facts

Name
Iowa Family Integrity and Patient Protection Initiative
Founder
Gina Lyn Cox — Altoona, Iowa (patient-rights advocate)
Core Goal
Protect lawful medical-cannabis patients from discrimination in child-custody, foster-care, and adoption proceedings.
Standard
Evidence of impairment, not the mere presence of THC metabolites.
Scope
Legislative amendments • Iowa HHS guidance • Judicial education

Core Workstreams

Legislative Reform

Clarify Iowa’s family-law and child-welfare language so parents aren’t judged by metabolites. Draft text emphasizes evidence-based safety tests.

Iowa HHS Guidance

Issue consistent administrative guidance to align assessments, case notes, and safety plans with state medical-cannabis law.

Judicial Education

Provide a concise bench memo: when toxicology is relevant, what “impairment” evidence looks like, and how to weigh expert testimony.

FAQ

Does this change mandatory reporting or safety standards?

No. It clarifies that the correct standard is impairment and objective safety risk, not a positive test alone.

Is recreational cannabis involved?

No. This initiative addresses lawful patients within Iowa’s medical program. It does not expand eligibility or possession limits.

How does this affect caseworkers and the courts?

It reduces ambiguity. Caseworkers get updated guidance; judges get a short reminder to evaluate impairment and evidence, not metabolites.
